# Analysis Plan — Food Delivery App Satisfaction Survey

## Overview

This survey captures 25 respondents' experiences with food delivery apps. The goal is to understand who uses these apps, which apps they prefer, how satisfied they are, and what drives that satisfaction. Respondents under 18 were screened out, and those who don't use food delivery apps did not answer the main questions.

---

## 1. Sample Summary

Before diving into insights, establish a clear picture of who is in the data.

- How many respondents completed the survey in full?
- How many were screened out (too young, or don't use food delivery apps)?
- What is the gender and age breakdown of the qualified sample?

This sets the base numbers for everything that follows.

---

## 2. App Usage & Market Reach

Look at which food delivery apps people are using (Q2).

- Which app has the widest reach — used by the most respondents?
- How many people use just one app versus multiple apps?
- Are there common combinations (e.g., people who use both GrabFood and ShopeeFood)?

This gives a competitive landscape view across the apps in the market.

---

## 3. Ordering Behavior

Understand how frequently people order (Q3) and how much they spend per month (Q5).

- What is the most common ordering frequency — daily, weekly, occasional?
- What is the average monthly spend? Is there a wide range, or do most people spend similarly?
- Do heavy users (daily / several times a week) spend significantly more than occasional users?

---

## 4. What People Value Most

Look at the attributes respondents said they care about most (Q4).

- Which attribute ranks highest overall — price, speed, variety, ease of use, or reviews?
- Is there a clear winner, or are preferences spread out?
- Do different demographic groups prioritize different things (e.g., younger users care more about price; older users about reviews)?

---

## 5. Satisfaction

Overall satisfaction (Q6) is the central metric of this survey.

- What is the overall satisfaction level? Focus on the "Top 2 Box" — the combined share of "Very satisfied" and "Satisfied."
- Does satisfaction differ by gender or age group?
- Are users of certain apps more satisfied than others?
- Do heavier users report higher or lower satisfaction than light users?
- Is there a link between monthly spend and satisfaction — do higher spenders feel they get better value?

---

## 6. What Drives Satisfaction

Connect the valued attributes (Q4) back to satisfaction (Q6).

- Among people who prioritize delivery speed, are they more satisfied than those who prioritize price?
- Which attribute, when a respondent values it, is most associated with high satisfaction?

This helps identify which aspects of the service matter most for keeping customers happy.

---

## 7. Recommendation Likelihood

Look at whether users would recommend food delivery apps to others (Q7).

- What share say "Yes definitely" vs. "Yes possibly" vs. "No"?
- How closely does recommendation track with satisfaction — are satisfied users nearly always willing to recommend?
- Are there any segments (gender, age, app used) where users are satisfied but still reluctant to recommend?

---

## 8. Demographic Differences

Check whether gender, age, and education level shape behavior and attitudes in meaningful ways.

- Do men and women differ in which apps they use or how satisfied they are?
- Are younger respondents ordering more frequently than older ones?
- Does education level relate to monthly spend or satisfaction?

---

## Key Questions to Answer

- What is the incidence rate of food delivery app usage among the surveyed population?
- Which app leads in reach and which has the most satisfied users?
- What drives satisfaction — and what is most likely to make users churn or recommend?
- Are there underserved segments (e.g., a demographic group with notably lower satisfaction)?
